Morality and Ethics

While some dismiss morality as a dangerous and subjective endeavor, not all moral frameworks are equal. The moral philosophy of Voluntaryism stands as a beacon of clarity and simplicity. At its core, Voluntaryism champions the fundamental principle that all human interaction must be entirely consensual and non-coercive. It uniquely focuses its evaluative lens almost exclusively on the initiation of force or fraud, liberating us from arbitrary rules.

Voluntaryism does not dictate personal choices, lifestyles, or beliefs, no matter how unconventional, as long as they do not infringe upon the equal freedom of others. A true moral principle must be universally applicable and unchanging, a steadfast axiom guiding all interaction. While countless institutions relentlessly seek to obfuscate this truth, seducing us into accepting that some humans possess rights they do not, we recognize this illusion for what it is. The current state of human emergency, fueled by compulsory taxes and regulations, demands a pioneering solution—a life forged from genuine consent, not coercion.

The Illusion of Delegated Rights

Most are convinced that the rules governing our current society are immutable, even believing that unjust laws must be blindly obeyed and enforced. This way of thinking is rooted in a palpable fear of repercussion, a fear intensified by the absence of a widely known and proven pathway toward a life unfettered by coercive dictates. While this fear is understandable, it is a barrier we are actively dismantling.

You inherently own yourself, possessing the undeniable right to choose with whom you will associate, to whom you will pay tribute, or to what you will be subjected. This simple truth forms the bedrock of our moral code. You cannot delegate rights you do not possess; no individual or group can ever legitimately decide what another person consents to. Yet, in this society, many tragically pretend to delegate these non-existent "rights" to individuals known as "elected officials," enforced by "police," and upheld by a monopolized injustice system.

Acknowledging these stark facts can evoke complex emotions, but crucially, it does not mean we are powerless to change this world for the better. We choose to rally around a common cause—Freedom—rather than against a common enemy. To us, freedom is not merely about escaping monstrous systems; it is profoundly measured by your unhindered ability to conduct your consensual, voluntary affairs as you see fit, without fear of illegitimate repercussions. Our lifestyle, at an individual level, liberates us from many unnecessary taxes and regulations, and our unwavering goal is to pioneer, provision access to, and foster a parallel network—a truly free way of life and counter-economy of morally-conscious individuals.

The Perpetual Engine

We are not creating the network itself; the Network already exists, though currently dispersed and small. Instead, envision us as Network Support, dedicating our entire lives and livelihoods to forging stronger bonds among existing network participants. We foster a larger vision for how we can holistically address the needs of our decentralized, freedom-supporting communities and markets in every conceivable way.

A peaceful society is achievable, founded on a truly simple concept: no individual should aggress upon another or their property. All agreements and associations between individuals or groups must be purely voluntary and consensual. No person or group should ever be granted the 'freedom' to aggress. Justice is rooted in restitution, ensuring victims can exact compensation from their aggressors. Furthermore, individuals targeted by aggression must never be prevented from exercising defensive force in opposition. We identify these as the Non-Aggression Principle (NAP), the Self-Defense Principle (SDP), and the Principle of Restitution (PoR). We can pave the way for an organic, voluntary society based solely on these precepts, but this demands more than merely encouraging the spread of voluntaryist culture in immediate surroundings. Even small voluntaryist communities cannot create a truly free society in the larger world unless they share a broader vision for freedom beyond their own domain and actively join to create the indispensable network of protected free markets.

Our Role

We are not the network itself, nor does its materialization depend on any single individual. However, our Caravan fulfills a critically important role within this larger ecosystem. While a majority of the people we encounter aspire to settle on property and cultivate their own food—an endeavor of undeniable soundness—one of their biggest struggles is finding a consistent market for their goods and services, both within and beyond their homestead.

Farming and producing goods are profoundly desirable endeavors, offering a direct connection to one's efforts to exist in a healthy and positive way, unlike earning money from unrelated tasks. The challenge lies in reaching new customers when farms are often distant from major cities, and the substantial costs of transportation can drastically reduce profits and consume valuable time. As full-time nomads, our operational model inherently subsidizes these travel costs through the sheer volume of goods we transport. For example, we strategically incorporate rideshare and package delivery to optimize our travels, ensuring every movement generates value.
