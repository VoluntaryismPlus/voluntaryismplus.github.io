The Intentional Partnership

Worksheet Seven: Aligning Our Daily Lives

Complete this worksheet especially if you are in a couple.

Our seminar highlights that "every minutia of day-to-day actions" reflects on the partnership. This worksheet would help a couple ensure their daily habits and routines are in service of their shared values and goals. It turns everyday tasks into opportunities for connection and collaboration.

Contents:

Daily Check-In: Prompts to reflect on how a day's actions contributed to or detracted from the shared vision.

Division of Labor: A space to honestly assess and discuss the workload in the relationship, from chores to emotional labor.

Building Positive Rituals: Prompts to identify and commit to small daily or weekly rituals that strengthen their connection and reinforce their commitment.

Priorities Map: What are each of our priorities and how do each of these get met by these different actions?

This worksheet is designed to help you and your partner consciously bring your shared values and vision into the everyday fabric of your relationship. By reflecting on and intentionally shaping your daily routines, you can ensure that even the smallest actions reinforce your commitment and build a more harmonious partnership.

1. Our Daily Rhythms & Connection Points

This section helps you map out your typical day and identify moments where you can strengthen your bond, even amid busy schedules.

Our Typical Weekday/Weekend:

On a standard weekday,

What time would I like to typically wake up? Go to bed?

What are my individual key activities (work, hobbies, appointments)?

What time would I like to typically have meals?

What times would I like to interact? (e.g., morning coffee, dinner, evening wind-down)

On a standard weekend, how do our rhythms shift?

Prompt: "Reflect on a 'typical' day or week. What are the key times we're together, and what are we usually doing? Are there times we're often disconnected, even if we're in the same space?"

Compare notes with your partner and talk about how to move forward in a way that maximizes both partners needs being met.

Desired Connection Rituals:

Identify one or two small, intentional rituals you'd like to integrate into your daily or weekly routine to foster connection. These could be brief moments of focused attention, shared activities, or check-ins.

Examples: "Morning coffee together before starting work," "A 10-minute 'download' after dinner," "A weekly 'date night' for just us."

Prompt: "What small, consistent actions can we commit to that will make us feel more connected each day or week?"

2. Our Responsibilities & Contributions

This section addresses the practical aspects of shared living, ensuring fairness and clear expectations, reflecting the idea that everyone's actions affect the partnership.

Household & Life Management Tasks:

List all recurring tasks required to manage your household and lives (e.g., cooking, cleaning, bills, pet care, grocery shopping, emotional labor, planning social events, vehicle maintenance). Next to each one, write who completes the task and any notes on how you feel about this division.

Prompt: "Let's list all the ongoing responsibilities in our shared life. Who currently handles what, and how do we feel about this division?"

Assessing Fairness & Needs:

For each task, discuss whether the current division feels fair and sustainable for both partners.

Identify any areas where one partner feels overwhelmed or where contributions are unclear.

Prompt: "Are there any tasks or areas where one of us feels overwhelmed, unappreciated, or where clarity is needed?"

Adjusting Our Contributions:

Based on your discussion, decide on any shifts or reassignments of tasks that would create a more balanced and supportive dynamic.

Prompt: "What adjustments can we make to our responsibilities to ensure both our needs are met and our contributions feel equitable and appreciated?"

3. Living Our Shared Values

This final section helps ensure that your everyday choices and activities reflect the "Initial Agreement" and the broader vision you have for your life together.

Connecting Actions to Values:

Recall your shared vision and core values from the "Building Our Shared Vision" worksheet (e.g., health, financial security, adventure, community, personal growth, connection).

Prompt: "Looking at our daily and weekly activities, which ones clearly reflect our shared values? Which ones might be subtly working against them?"

Intentional Daily Choices:

Identify one or two specific, actionable changes you can make in your daily routines or spending habits that would better align with your shared values and long-term vision.

Examples: "Cooking healthy meals at home (health)," "Saving a specific amount each week (financial security)," "Scheduling regular walks together (connection/health)."

Prompt: "What small, intentional choice can we each make this week that directly reinforces one of our shared values or contributes to our long-term vision?"

Review & Adapt:

Set a time to revisit this worksheet, perhaps monthly or quarterly, to discuss how well you're aligning your daily lives and make any necessary adjustments.

Prompt: "When shall we check in again on our daily alignment to ensure we're staying on track and adapting as our lives evolve?"

This structured approach encourages ongoing communication and conscious effort, transforming daily routines into powerful expressions of your shared commitment.
