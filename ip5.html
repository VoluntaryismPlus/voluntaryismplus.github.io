Step Five: The Initial Agreement

Now that you have established your relationship dynamic, it's time to make your initial agreement.

Here is our initial agreement with each element broken down and explained.

The Initial Agreement

Before I enter a relationship with you, I will make sure that the following is true for me.

Relationships started without a good amount of deliberation are often suffering from a lack of communication and skewed expectations. Sometimes people are trying to be desirable and so they hide parts of themselves that they think are less attractive. This is bound to end badly because then your partner doesn't know who they are in a relationship with.

If you are already in a relationship, you can still salvage it if both partners are willing. Your partner might be excited to learn who you really are. You may want to reflect on what would have been different if you had taken these steps before making it official. We encourage you to forgive yourself and your partner for any mistakes made because of a lack of clarity when the relationship started.

You can always refresh the relationship with a new agreement that serves both people better.

1. I do not believe that love is a feeling that can go away, but a choice. I take responsibility for the healthy interpretation and resolution of any feelings I have as long as you are also willing to work with me on the root causes and results of these feelings.

If love was a feeling, it could be possible to wake up one morning and not be in love any more. We have noticed that when this supposedly happens, it is either because something in the relationship is not working, or because the person who falls out of love believes that love is a feeling that can go away randomly like when you stop liking a certain song.

This is one of many beliefs that can ruin a relationship. More of these beliefs are covered in Step Six.

2. I acknowledge the relationship dynamic we have agreed upon and I will work to maintain my promises.

3. I am willing to make changes to my way of doing things, or to myself, in order to be a better partner for you.

4. I promise that if anything comes up that threatens our relationship, like:
- I am changing
- I am discovering new things about myself
- I am discovering more about you
- I have met someone who I like
- I am commiserating with others

I will tell you as soon as I realize it, before I make any new conclusions that bring us farther apart, and I will give you the opportunity to be involved in any conclusions I make.

5. I have a method by which any problems can be addressed, and I do not believe these experiences have power over us but that we have the power to use the learning they hold to make us stronger.
