Step Five: Choosing the Right Person


Here is another unpopular opinion we have:

You can make a promise to be with someone forever and be prepared to keep it!

You must try to ascertain before getting into a relationship with someone whether or not they are the right person for you, and don’t make any promises until you know.

This takes time, and we have all been fooled, but we have a method by which you could be more certain:

Good Partner Checklist
  • See if they’re willing to talk about anything you find important
  • See if they are devoted to spending more time with you than is socially acceptable.
  • See if they are willing to change for you based on your needs.
  • See if they follow through with these behaviors over time, and if they keep their promises.

Reflect upon these questions:

  • Are you the biggest event in this person's life, in their opinion?
  • Can you tell them everything?

Who to enter a relationship with:

Check For Compatibility
  • Overall goals in life
  • Achievements
  • What makes you feel successful
  • Making money
  • Social goals
  • Desires for lifestyle
  • Location
  • Shelter type
  • Cost of living
  • Desired relationship dynamics
  • Workload
  • Chores
  • Time together
  • Conversation amount
  • Physical needs
  • Admiration
  • Someone who you don’t see any deal breaker problems with
  • Someone you don’t have animosity towards
  • Someone whose family dynamic you enjoy
  • If you want kids, someone who would be a good parent
  • Someone who makes your desired impressions socially
How to enter into a relationship with real promises
  • Time

    - Taking time to know all sides of the person

    - Being in different settings with them

    - Seeing if they desire another person while you wait
  • Self-knowledge

    - Potential downfalls

    - Have to be willing to work actively against those downfalls
  • Have a plan for working against them:

    - Stopping them from happening

    - Stopping them from progressing
How to Maintain a Good Relationship
  • Updating expectations
  • Dispelling the honeymoon period myth
  • Dispelling the myth of boredom or variety
  • Addressing issues
  • Avoiding stagnation
Follow these Guidelines:
  • Always Talk About Everything
  • Set Up For Success:

    - Make the relationship one that you want to respect above your own personal desires

    - Account for people’s desires

In a partnership, what each person does reflect back on the partnership. This applies to every minutia of day-to-day actions. There’s no denying that these person is affected by the other person decisions, whether that affect is small or large, positive negative or neutral.

Every action I do is a representation of our relationship. If I feel something will go against my calling, and that it interferes with what is agreed-upon in the relationship, I will try to find a way to talk about it and to reach a conclusion where I don’t have to go against the relationship.

In this way, I put the relationship first. I check in with the relationship Before I take any action because I know that alchemically speaking, there is an agreement that I’ve made to respect the relationship and its values and boundaries.
