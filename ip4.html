Inter-Connectivity

In a partnership, what each person does affects the other partner. This applies to every minutia of day-to-day actions. There’s no denying that each person is affected by the other person's decisions, whether the effect is small or large, positive, negative or neutral.

If there are actions one partner takes that bother the other partner, they need to be brought up right away. Each partner has the right to know how their actions affect the other person.

Every action I do is a representation of our relationship. If I feel something I want interferes with what is agreed-upon in the relationship, I will talk about it and try to reach a conclusion where I don’t have to go against the relationship.

In this way, I put the relationship first. I check in with the relationship before I take any action. This is because I know that there is an agreement that I’ve made to respect the relationship and its values and boundaries.

A partnership is more than two people coexisting—it's a single, functioning unit. While Steps One through Three are about individual readiness and choosing the right person, Step Four is about how you operate together. This is where you create the deliberate dynamic that will either help your relationship thrive under pressure or fall apart.

In this step, you will move beyond individual thought and create a shared operating manual for your relationship. Here’s how you can begin to build that strong, collaborative dynamic.

1. How Do You Make Decisions Together?

A relationship dynamic can be defined by how it handles decisions, from the small to the monumental. Without a clear method, a simple choice about dinner can spiral into a power struggle. This step is about establishing a shared process.

Define Your Roles:

Some decisions may fall more naturally to one partner, such as managing investments, while others require joint input. It’s crucial to have a clear understanding of these roles.

Create a Process:

For decisions that impact both of you, establish a consistent process. This could be a scheduled discussion, a specific way you weigh pros and cons, or a method for respectfully disagreeing and reaching a consensus.

The Goal is a Win-Win:

The objective is always to find a solution that works for the partnership, not just for one person.

2. Ensuring Communication

Communication is the lifeblood of your relationship. But it can’t be a passive activity that only happens when a problem arises. It must be a proactive priority.

Scheduled Check-ins:

Establish dedicated time—daily, weekly, or monthly—to talk without distractions. Use this time not just for logistics, but to check in on each other’s emotional well-being and the health of the relationship itself.

Learn Your Partner's Language:

Pay close attention to how your partner communicates, not just what they say. Understand their personal definitions of words and their body language. The goal is to deeply comprehend their perspective.

Create a Safe Space:

The foundation of all communication is safety. Agree that you can both be honest and vulnerable without fear of harsh judgment or retaliation.

3. Putting the Relationship First

This is the golden rule of a healthy dynamic. It's the commitment that when push comes to shove, you will always prioritize the health of the relationship over individual pride or personal convenience.

Us vs. the Problem:

The instant you see a conflict as "me versus you," you've already lost. A strong dynamic views every challenge as a shared problem to be solved together.

Protect Your Connection:

A healthy dynamic protects the relationship from external forces, whether it's the stress of work, the influence of family, or the temptation of others.

The Partnership is Your Shared Resource:

A healthy relationship is a resource that both of you contribute to and benefit from. Putting it first means making choices that keep that resource strong and resilient, ensuring it can support both of you through anything.

By intentionally creating these clear agreements and practices, you are not just building a life together—you are building a deliberate, resilient, and enduring dynamic.
