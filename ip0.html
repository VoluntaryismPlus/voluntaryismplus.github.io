The Intentional Partnership

Method for Addressing Changes


When major changes threaten a relationship, there is always an opportunity for the couple to grow closer instead of growing apart.


However, if one partner's values change, there needs to be a re-assessment of the relationship because they are effectively a different person from the one who made the initial agreement.


Each of these changes provides a grave challenge to the initial agreement. They may result in termination of the agreement by either party. But there is always a way to resolve any issues resulting from change if that is the desired path.


Issue: I am changing

Description:

I don’t know if I want to remain as I was when we met. I want to start living in a different way, doing different things, or being a different person.


Solution:

Change is good. Sometimes it is scary when you don’t know the result of where the change will lead you. But there will never be a final destination. The relationship needs to be flexible enough to account for these changes.

If I am changing, it is unlikely that my basic convictions will change. But if they do, we will have to make a new agreement. You will have the opportunity to opt out of making a new agreement.


Suggestion:

Let yourself and your partner get to know the new you. Talk candidly about the effects these changes will make, making extra sure that you address how this will change things for your partner. Brainstorm ideas for how you can make this transition positive for them as well as you.

Issue: I am discovering new things about myself

Description:

I might not have known before something about myself. That thing changes who I am, which changes our agreements.


Solution:

It is no need for alarm when someone realizes they are different from what they thought they were. There is always something more to learn about yourself. As long as you take account of what this changes for your partner and they agree to the updated agreement, the relationship will survive.


Suggestion:

Involve your partner in this process and make it into an opportunity to grow closer. Bring them along on the journey of self discovery and know that you can always choose how to interpret the outcome of these revelations, and therefore any choice that brings you farther from your relationship is avoidable. Remember, the relationship's agreement is not based on a feeling but based on mutual respect and commitment.

Issue: I am discovering more about you

Description:

I am seeing sides of you I may not have signed up for. I thought you were one way, but your actions seem to display a different thing.


Solution:

I need to get clarity and may suspend my agreement until I can re-commit to the relationship.


Suggestion:

Ask your partner for clarity with regards to any values or commitments you are concerned might be jeapordized by your partner's revealed self. Think of ways they can reassure you and reconfirm any tenets of your relationship that you are worried might change.

Issue: I have met someone who I like

Description:

Someone I met or already know is becoming attractive to me. There is something lacking for me in the relationship and I have found a hint about it in another person. It usually stems from the idea that this person could provide me with a different experience than I get currently. It may be that I see something in them that reminds me of a part of myself who isnt expressed with you for some reason or another.


Solution:

If my partner is willing to work with me to find a way for this desire to be met by my partner or this part of me to be expressed with my partner, we can learn and grow stronger from this occurance.


Suggestion:

Consider that the person you have become attracted to is both more and less than your perception of them. More in that they are their own whole person with their own set of contracts they are or are not upholding. Less in that the version of them you percieve is idealized, and they are likely not what you see them as. Take this as a safe opportunity to separate the idealized version from the person themselves and see how you and your partner can embody those traits you value, which have been gifted to you from this interaction.

Issue: I am commiserating with others about you

Description:

I am finding it hard to resolve issues with you and I turned to someone else or multiple people and talked about you to them. My intention was to to get perspective, blow off steam, etc.


Solution:

I will tell you as soon as possible about this occurance and work to regain your trust as well as resolve the issue with you as soon as posible, refraining from introducing myself to situations where I may be tempted to commiserate again until all issues are resolved. This may involve resolution with anyone I involved in a conversation about my partner.


Suggestion:

Have compassion for the reasoning that lead you to talk to others. Make a plan to stop the chain of events that leads to these conversations before it gets to that point. There might be some underlying feelings that need to be adressed.
