The Intentional Partnership

A lasting, loving relationship isn’t something you stumble into by accident. It's something you build intentionally. It requires a shared vision, a common language, and a proactive method for navigating challenges.

Most couples wait for problems to arise before addressing them. Our approach is different: we believe in creating a strong, deliberate dynamic from the start. We believe that love is not just a feeling—it’s a commitment to a process.

Here is a glimpse into our six-step method for building a resilient relationship.

Our Method for a Lasting Partnership

  • Step One: Becoming Ready
    Before you can fully commit to someone else, you must commit to yourself. This step is about honestly assessing your own strengths and weaknesses, understanding your core values, and knowing your non-negotiable traits. It’s the essential foundation for a healthy partnership.

  • Step Two: Knowing What You Want
    This step helps you clarify your personal stance on key issues, free from external pressures and common myths. We challenge outdated beliefs about topics like spending all your time together, lifelong commitments, and the idea that a single person can’t meet all of your needs.

  • Step Three: Choosing the Right Person
    Building a strong relationship starts with a compatible partner. We provide a framework to help you take the time to truly get to know someone and to evaluate your compatibility before making a long-term commitment.

  • Step Four: Relationship Dynamics
    This step focuses on how you operate as a team. You will create clear agreements on how you'll make decisions together, how you'll ensure communication is a priority, and how you'll make sure the health of the relationship always comes first.

  • Step Five: The Initial Agreement
    This is where all the previous work comes together. You and your partner will formalize your shared vision and relationship dynamics into a clear, actionable Initial Agreement—the cornerstone of your partnership.

  • Step Six: Maintaining a Good Relationship
    A healthy relationship is a living thing that requires regular care. This step is about proactively updating expectations as you both grow and continue to challenge common myths, such as the inevitability of boredom. We believe a relationship can become more exciting and fulfilling over time, not less.
