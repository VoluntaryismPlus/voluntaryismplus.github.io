What Collaboration Actually Looks Like

Option 1: Guest Content & Speaking

Time commitment: Low | Impact: Medium

  • Speak at one of our festivals (1-2 hour workshop)
  • Write guest articles for our documentation
  • Record video content for guild training
  • Participate in Q&A sessions with the community

Best for: Established thought leaders who want to reach a new, action-oriented audience

Option 2: Framework Contribution

Time commitment: Medium | Impact: High

  • Develop implementation guides for your area of expertise
  • Create templates others can use and adapt
  • Document best practices from your experience
  • Help refine our existing frameworks

Best for: Practitioners who've solved specific problems and want others to benefit from their solutions

Option 3: Guild Leadership

Time commitment: High | Impact: Very High

  • Lead or co-lead a guild in your area of expertise
  • Develop curriculum and training programs
  • Mentor apprentices and emerging practitioners
  • Build infrastructure for your specific domain

Best for: Experts ready to pour significant energy into building something lasting

Option 4: Strategic Partnership

Time commitment: Variable | Impact: Multiplied

  • Integrate our network with your existing community
  • Co-develop new initiatives or programs
  • Share resources, audiences, and infrastructure
  • Build complementary systems that reinforce each other

Best for: Leaders of established movements or organizations looking to add practical implementation capacity

How to Start a Conversation

Step 1: Initial Contact

Email us at caravanarchy@proton.me with:

  • Brief intro to who you are and what you do
  • Your area of expertise or interest
  • What type of collaboration you're considering
  • What problem you're trying to solve (if applicable)

Step 2: Exploratory Call

We'll set up a conversation to:

  • Understand your goals and vision
  • Explore alignment with our network
  • Identify concrete collaboration opportunities
  • Determine if this makes sense for both sides

Step 3: Pilot Project

Start small with a defined project:

  • One workshop at a festival, or
  • One article/guide contribution, or
  • One joint event or initiative

This lets us work together before making bigger commitments.

Step 4: Ongoing Collaboration

If the pilot works, we explore deeper integration:

  • Regular speaking engagements
  • Guild leadership or advisory role
  • Strategic partnership development
  • Co-creation of new initiatives

What We're Not Looking For

To be clear about our boundaries:

We're Not Looking for Influencers

If your main value is a large following but no practical expertise, this probably isn't the right fit. We need people who can teach, build, and implement‚Äînot just broadcast.

We're Not Looking for Control

If you want to lead the whole network or have everyone adopt your specific vision, that won't work. We're building a decentralized ecosystem where many visions can coexist and cross-pollinate.

We're Not Looking for Pure Theory

If you can't connect your ideas to practical implementation, they won't be useful here. We respect theory, but we need frameworks that work in the real world with real people.

We're Not Looking for Compromise

If your approach requires working with the state, seeking political solutions, or compromising on voluntary principles, we're not aligned. This is explicitly an agorist network.

Your Ideas Deserve Implementation

You've put years into developing your frameworks, building your audience, refining your vision. But without practical infrastructure, it stays theoretical.

We're building that infrastructure. And we're doing it in a way that doesn't require compromising principles, seeking political power, or waiting for permission.

Let's build it together.
