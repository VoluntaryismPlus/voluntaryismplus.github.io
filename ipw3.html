Word Definitions

Here is how I use and define the following terms in conversation:

Think - “I have made a conclusion based on perceived facts”

This is a potential logical conclusion I have some amount of suspicion about.

Feel - “I had this reaction, but the reaction does not reflect my logical thinking.”

I have a sensation or reaction that I don’t necessarily attribute to logical conclusions based on facts.

Less blame, because my feeling is not asserting facts beyond the fact of an emotional response or sensation.

Replace These Phrases:

These are phrases that, when used, often create misunderstandings in those listening. We have specifically chosen these phrases because they are not necessary and should only be used in specific instances.

You Always/Never:

Rephrasing:

It seems to me as if ____ is a recurring behavior.

I have felt as if ____ was the case multiple times now.

I feel like you always:

Subjective interpretation. There are patterns that create the sense within me that this happens

Example:

“I feel like you always tell me my opinion is wrong”

Vs.

You always:

Based on Facts. I am certain that this is what’s happening.

Example:

“you always get home 30 minutes later than you say you will”
