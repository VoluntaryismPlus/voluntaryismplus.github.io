The Intentional Partnership

Step One: Becoming Prepared

There are several steps that we insist any person should undertake if they are interested in joining a partnership that will last forever.

Are You Ready?

Everyone deserves love. But if you’re not prepared to be in a relationship, you shouldn’t be in one. Luckily, you can prepare yourself. Becoming prepared for a lasting relationship is not some far away goal that will only come from years of meditation, practice and study.

This first step can be used as a personal check-in about your forever relationship readiness.

Forever? Really?

If you are worried about the term "forever", you might have never found someone who you felt deserved to be loved forever. Why would we say 'deserved'? We use the term 'deserving' to denote someone who is prepared to be responsible for themselves in relationship. This means they will not be in a relationship that they do not see as 'forever' material. This means that they know what they want and do not hold beliefs that are toxic to relationships.

How to Become Deserving of a Life Partner:
  1. Be a person who could love someone for life - Examine judgments - Do you think you are above all others? - Going too far with beliefs - If you are going to judge, make sure they have the same dogmatism for life
  2. Be aware of and communicate your non-negotiables

There are some traits we have identified as marking someone as being prepared for a relationship. Here are some of these:

Non-Negotiable Traits:
  1. Openness
  2. Communication
  3. Self-awareness
  4. Interest in your partner
  5. Open minded about all of your partner’s interests and habits (You can still want them to change)
  6. Responsibilities around you and your partner's needs
  7. You can work with them
  8. They should know their needs, but you can help them discover them
  9. Interest in helping them know themselves more

I will use the terms “good” and “bad” here. My reasoning is that most good and bad is subjective, and something that you have to assess for yourself. What would be a healthy direction for you to go towards? That is your good. Not moving towards anything personally healthy is your bad.

Therefore, since you can learn from any action or decision, I would assert that no actions or decisions are bad in themselves, and that it would only be a move in a “bad” direction to not learn from the results in a way that supports your “good” path.

Even more controversially, I would say that there are some things that are objectively good or bad. I would argue that conducting voluntary interactions with others is good, and force or coercion is bad. Change through learning is good, and stagnation is bad. Searching for the truth to a situation is good, and self-deception or willful ignorance is bad. Having a toxic and powerless relationship with a family member, substance or habit is bad.

Note that most inherently bad things involve a person first being aware that they are making these bad decisions. They need to be aware that there is another option. If you make these bad decisions continuously even when made aware of them, you are most likely not going to be good for someone in a relationship.

Know Your Patterns

In order to plan for how to maintain a respectful relationship, you must set it up for the most success possible. Think of solutions to your most common pitfalls in relationships and in life. Having someone who cares about you in your life could create an opening for new ways of addressing issues.

We do not define these patterns as weaknesses, but instead as patterns which are strategies based on needs.

However you address your patterns, this is a crucial step in being present for a bond that is designed not to fail.

Depression

If you are often depressed or anxious, make sure to involve your partner as a positive force in those situations. Do not set yourself up for failure by ignoring the possibility that you might become depressed regardless of whether your partner is fulfilling their agreement with you.

Instead, tell your partner about the things that trigger your depression and make sure they are willing to help support you to address the situations that trigger these feelings. Consider that your depression might be situational, and that it might be trying to tell you something about a deep desire you have, or a deep fear you have not adressed. Allow your partner to be on the same team as you as you work on your life to right this wrong.

Infidelity

If you are tempted to look for others outside of your relationship, have compassion for that part of yourself and discover what it is that you are really looking for when you do this. Are you looking for excitement? If so, how can you make sure your relationship will excite you?

Alternatively, you might have a world-view that creates these problems. Do you believe that love is a feeling that fades naturally over time? Do you believe that if someone got close to you, they would never love you fully? Perhaps that you are inherently a bad person? Or do you believe that no one deserves enduring, faithful love? You should realize that any of these beliefs are false and that you should never enter a relationship while holding any of them as true.

Do you want more from a partner than you feel comfortable asking for? Perhaps you aren't fulfilled by a partner because they do not give you the love in the way you desire and deserve. To learn more about how to ask for the love you desire, see Step Four.

Boredom

It is not your partner's responsibility to entertain you, but you need to choose a person who has a lifestyle that excites you. Even if you are excited by being a homebody, there will always be certain activities that make you fulfilled and happy. You need to identify these before entering a relationship.

The need for entertainment can stem from a life lacking in meaning. Your daily life should be designed to fulfil you such that excitement comes from achieving real things that make your life better. If you are dealing with a life you just want to cope with, that is an overarching problem that will cause all kinds of dysfunction in your life.

Conclusion

It's not true that all relationships lose their excitement over time. It is true that they may begin to devolve if one partner has expectations that the other partner does not reciprocate. That is why communication before entering a relationship is imperative. Talk about what you want your relationship to look like in five years. Make sure your partner wants something similar. But also make sure they are capable of this and that they know their own weaknesses as well as you do.
