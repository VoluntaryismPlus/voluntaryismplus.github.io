The Intentional Partnership

Step One: The Initial Agreement


We built this framework based on the positive results of the experiment of our relationship. Before we started our relationship, I took three months to align myself correctly and formulate a contract that I saw no way that I could break.

This was borne of a lifetime of looking outside of my romantic relationships for fulfillment and understanding. I took this time and created this contract because I needed to know that I could promise I would not falter because of what I found lacking in our relationship.

I figured out an agreement that I could commit to, but it required my partner to also be open to changing himself, his habits, and his worldview, especially if I had a problem that needed to be addressed.

I did not eliminate the possibility of a breakup, but I lessened it dramatically by addressing all of my most common pitfalls. Importantly, I put the survival of our relationship into both of our hands, ensuring that if my partner was willing to do what was needed to make it survive, I was willing to do the same.

This agreement is based on my own personal weaknesses. Therefore, another person's agreement might have a few unique clauses to this one. My agreement can either be used as a guide or you are welcome to use it verbatim.

The Initial Agreement

Before I enter a relationship with you, I will make sure that the following is true for me.

  1. I do not believe that love is a feeling that can go away, but a choice. I take responsibility for the healthy interpretation and resolution of any feelings I have as long as you are also willing to work with me on the root causes and results of these feelings.
  2. I am willing to make changes to my way of doing things, or to myself, in order to be a better partner for you.
  3. I promise that if anything comes up that threatens our relationship, like:
    - I am changing
    - I am discovering new things about myself
    - I am discovering more about you
    - I have met someone who I like
    - I am commiserating with others

    I will tell you as soon as I realize it, before I make any new conclusions that bring us farther apart, and I will give you the opportunity to be involved in any conclusions I make.
  4. I have a method by which any problems can be addressed, and I do not believe these experiences have power over us but that we have the power to use them to make us stronger.

We will provide our method to address these problems on the next page.
